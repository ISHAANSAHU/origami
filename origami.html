THESE ARE SOME AWESOME ORAGAMIES YOU CAN LEARN TO MAKE

CAMEL

intresting facts

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
CHAMALEON

intresting facts

  • THEIR FEET WORK LIKE SALAD TONGS. ... ALMOST HALF OF ALL KNOWN SPECIES LIVE IN MADAGASCAR. ... CHAMELEONS VARY WILDLY IN TERMS OF SIZE. ... THEY MAINLY CHANGE COLOR IN ORDER TO COMMUNICATE OR REGULATE BODY TEMPERATURE. ... SKIN CRYSTALS ENABLE THEM TO CHANGE COLOR AT WILL. ... UNLIKE MANY LIZARDS, CHAMELEONS CAN'T REGROW THEIR TAILS.
CICADA

intresting facts

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. ... The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus. ... They're true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). ... Most have red-orange eyes. ... Females may be attracted to the sound of motors. ... Their short adult life is not unusual for bugs.
PANDA

intresting facts

  • They have great camouflage for their environment. ... Their eyes are different to normal bears. ... Cubs are well protected in their first month. ... Courageous cubs! ... A helping hand. ... They spend a lot of their day eating. ... Bamboo is critical to their diet. ... But they do occasionally eat something other than bamboo.
PIGEON

intresting facts

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals. ... Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities. ... Pigeons are highly sociable animals. ... Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
TEDDY

intresting facts

  • The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip. Inspired by a cartoon featuring the event, shopkeeper Morris Michtom and his wife Rose made a stuffed bear with a sign 'Teddy's bear' and left it in their shop window.
